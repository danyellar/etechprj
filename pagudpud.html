Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte

Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte is often referred to as the Boracay of the North because of its exquisite white sands, many water sports (for those staying at private resorts), and crystal blue waters.

It is a simple municipality, unlike Boracay. This is not the place to go if you want to go shopping or go to a mall. There are no ATMs in the area, so bring cash with you when you go. Its Blue Lagoon beach is becoming more well-known, yet few visitors visit it yet.

When to Visit

The best time to visit Pagudpud in the Philippines is from January until May and November and December, when you will have a warm temperature and limited till mediocre rainfall. The highest average temperature in in Pagudpud is 30°C in April and the lowest is 27°C in January.

How to Get There

You can fly into Ilocos Norte's Laoag City and then book a bus or a car from there. Pagudpud is also included in many hotels and tour organizations' itineraries. Many tourists rent vehicles in this area because it is a pleasant spot to drive.

What To Do

The Bangui Windmills and the Kapurpurawan Rock Formation are worth seeing. Or simply unwind on the beach! Explore the old Spanish houses along Calle Crisologo in Vigan City, a few hours distant from Pagudpud. Consume the well-known Vigan empanada and longganisa! Vigan City is now officially recognized as one of the world's wonder cities, so be sure to add it to your itinerary.

What it is Known For

It has the same beautiful white sand as Boracay. It's also one of the best places in the Philippines for kitesurfing.
