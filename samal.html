Samal Island, Davao City

The Garden City of Samal is another name for Samal Island. It's about 2 kilometers beyond Davao City, Mindanao's capital, and 1400 kilometers from Manila.

It holds the distinction of being among the Philippines' most established holiday resorts. It features numerous white and pink sand beaches, and also world-class resorts such as Kaputian Beach Resort, the famed Pearl Farm, and Paradise Island.

You may select 70 numerous resorts on the island (or not the best, it depends on the budget you have). And it has a variety of coastal reefs and beautiful clear blue waters that are very soothing.

When to Visit

Anticipate overcrowding and humidity throughout peak season in August, and colder weather but much less visitors from December to February.

How to Get There

Travel to Davao City by plane. It is quite simple to go to the island via ferry from Davao City.

What To Do

Unwind at a resort or visit one of the many islands and coastlines. Also, keep an eye out for the vanishing island, which vanishes as the water rises.

What it is Known For

There are some very spectacular diving locations, as well as all the other fun activities that are associated with local life, such as trekking or relaxing on the beach. If you have the opportunity, explore Montfort Bats Conservation Park, which contains over 2 million fruit bats, garnering this a place in the Guinness Book of World Records for the many fruit bats in a cave. Mount Apo, the country's tallest mountain, is also worth witnessing. See the pink beach and enjoy the distinctive tropical fruits found exclusively in Davao.
