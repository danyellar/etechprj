El Nido, Palawan

El Nido, Palawan, is one of the Philippines' top beaches and island attractions, with "exceptional natural beauty and environment." It is sometimes referred to as the Philippines' last frontier and is placed No. 14 on CNN's list of the World's 100 Best Beaches. Palawan was named the "greatest island in the world" for the second year in a row (2016 and 2017) by the worldwide travel magazine Travel + Leisure. El Nido was also named the fourth-best beach in the world by Conde Nast Traveler in 2017.

When to Visit

The best time to visit El Nido, Palawan is from December through March because the weather is nice and dry and there are fewer visitors. Be prepared for more tourists and higher prices if you're traveling during Holy Week or high season (April and May).

How to Get There

(by air): For more private and high-end flights, Islands Transvoyager Inc. fly from Manila to El Nido directly. The small private airport in El Nido is only serviced by a chartered aircraft. The most popular way is to fly from Manila to Puerto Princesa, then drive to the tip of mainland Palawan. You’ll ride on an outrigger boat to reach parts of El Nido. SEAIR also flies from Manila to Taytay daily. You can also fly from Manila to Puerto Princesa, then take a four-hour bus ride to Taytay. (Update: There are now flights available from Puerto Princesa to El Nido and back, so it is now more convenient. Compared to a four- to five-hour van commute, a flight takes only 40 minutes.)

(by car): From San Jose Terminal in Puerto Princesa, there are buses bound for El Nido. Travel time is five to six hours.

What To Do

Scuba dive, snorkel, relax on the beach, visit a secret lagoon, eat bird’s nest soup, and take an island hopping tour.

What it is Known For

Bird’s nest soup, beautiful lagoons, limestone cliffs
