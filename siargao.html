Siargao Island, Surigao Del Norte

Surigao del Norte's Siargao Island is a tear-shaped island. It has Mindanao's largest mangrove forest, which is a major source of marine life. Diving, snorkeling, and, most importantly, surfing are all popular activities on the island.

It's a surfer's dream come true. Large waves form at Cloud Nine Reef, pushed by winds that run unabated over the Pacific Ocean, attracting thrill seekers from all over the world. The annual Siargao Cloud 9 Surfing Cup is held on this reef.

When to Visit

If you're not a surfer, don't visit between June and October. It's best to visit during the offseason or before the big waves arrive.

How to Get There

To get to Siargao Island, you either fly, use a boat or ferry, or rather take a bus.

What To Do

You can go surfing! Additionally, everything that has to do with nature. This is a hotspot for nature lovers, and unlike some of the other places, it is not swamped with hotels, bars, and stores. Game fishing is also popular.

What it is Known For

The Cloud 9 waves are well-known (the thick, hollow tubes that form at Cloud 9 Reef).
