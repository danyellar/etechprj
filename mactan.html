Mactan Island, Cebu

Cebu Island was recently voted sixth among the world's "greatest islands" by Travel + Leisure, an international travel magazine. Mactan Island, located in Cebu, is home to 430,000 people. It is the Philippines' most heavily populated island, with numerous tourist attractions and locations. Mactan, as a coral island, has a diverse marine ecosystem that is ideal for diving and snorkeling.

Because the island is made up of a variety of islets, you should take the opportunity to island hop while you're there. Jet skiing, sailing, and parasailing are just a few of the water sports available.

When to Visit

To avoid crowds, travel during the off-season. The warmest months are January through May, and the majority of the year is expected to be rainy. Cebu is one of the rainy locations in the Philippines, thus even the driest months get rain.

How to Get There

To get to Mactan, you either fly or take a ferry. Mactan Island is also linked to Cebu City by two bridges.

What To Do

Enjoy the resort lifestyle at one of numerous world-class resorts, explore the Olango Island Wildlife Sanctuary, or see the Visayas region's only aquarium.

What it is Known For

A popular all-year-round vacation spot with a wide range of international resorts to decide amongst. Also, explore other surrounding communities, as they have lots of good natural wonders such as waterfalls, lagoons, lakes, beaches, and mountains for outdoor enthusiasts.
