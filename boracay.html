Boracay, Aklan

Boracay is a small island in the province of Aklan. Boracay is one of the Philippines' most famous tourist attractions, known for its powdery white sands and clear blue waters. It offers approximately 350 resorts and 2,000 rooms, ranging from luxury hotels to affordable inns, to welcome visitors.

When to Visit

If you don't mind getting wet, go between November and May, though June through early October will be less crowded.

How to Get There

There are no direct flights into Boracay. To get there, you either need to fly into Caticlan (the closest airport) or Kalibo (an airport further away that sometimes offers cheaper flights). You’ll then need to take a vehicle to Caticlan Jetty port for the 10–20 minute boat ride to Boracay.

What To Do

If you're looking for some extreme sports, head to the island's east side for some kiting and windsurfing. Stay on the west side for a resort lifestyle and beach relaxation, then party it up at night.

What it is Known For

The White Beach, kite and windsurfing, scuba diving, snorkeling, helmet diving, nightlife
